Liquidity Pressure Is Rewiring
European Private Equity

European PE enters 2026 under a single defining pressure: the LP liquidity squeeze. Fundraising hit a decade low, distribution yields remain depressed, and fundraising timelines continue to stretch. But far from paralysis, sponsors are adapting — private credit is now a core liquidity tool, maturity walls are forcing proactive portfolio management, and continuation vehicles have emerged as a proven exit pathway. The playbook is changing fast.

1
Private Credit Becomes Core Financing Infrastructure
Global AUM hit $2.3tn — yet BSL volumes remain 2x the market, signalling substantial runway ahead.
Private credit has shifted from alternative to essential. Global AUM hit $2.3tn in 2025, forecast to reach $4.5tn by 2030. European direct lending volumes hit a record in 2025 — estimated at ~€40bn, surpassing full-year 2024 levels by November. Average spreads have tightened by over 100bps since 2020, with over a third of buyouts now priced below 500bps compared to none in 2023–24. Sponsors are increasingly using private credit not just for acquisitions, but for dividend recaps and liquidity extraction. Yet BSL volumes remain more than double the private credit market, signalling substantial room for continued structural growth.
Why It Matters
Financing relationships are now strategic infrastructure — not transactional tools. The recap surge shows private credit is a liquidity management lever, not just deal financing.
Signal
Recapitalisations reached ~11% of deal count in 2025 — the highest share on record.
PitchBook LCD Private Credit Monitor, Nov 2025
Implication
Treat direct lender relationships as core infrastructure. The alpha is in non-sponsored mid-market origination — European family businesses remain structurally underserved at a ~150bps return premium.

2
The Maturity Wall Is Shrinking — But Risk Is Concentrated
2028 wall cut by 36%, but 63% of what remains is rated CCC+ or lower. LMEs are migrating to Europe.
Refinancing activity in 2025 cut the 2028 European loan maturity wall by 36% — from €116.6bn to €74.4bn. But 63% of the remaining three-year maturity stack is rated CCC+ or lower. US-style liability management exercises have migrated to Europe: the combined default rate including LMEs reached 2.15% versus just 0.92% excluding them. Nearly a quarter of restructured loans have already been restructured before — a troubling 'repeat offender' pattern.
Why It Matters
Broad leverage stability at 5.25–6.0x masks severe sector-level stress. Chemicals, retail, and media — particularly in DACH — are where the cracks are widening.
Signal
DACH chemicals net leverage: 1.8x (2021) → 3.1x (2024). 48 facility downgrades vs just 18 upgrades in 2025.
AlixPartners, Oct 2025
Implication
Sponsors with 2021–22 vintage leveraged assets should manage maturity profiles now. Execute refinancings while windows remain open — H2 2026 conditions may tighten materially.

3
The LP Liquidity Squeeze Is Reshaping PE Behaviour
Fundraising at decade lows. CVs now 19% of exits — and outperforming buyouts on MOIC.
European PE fundraising in 2025 hit its lowest level in a decade — both in volume and quantum — while median fund closes stretched to 21.6 months. Distribution yields recovered from a Q1 2024 trough of 13.7% of NAV to 24.3%, but remain below any point in the prior decade. Sponsors are adapting: continuation vehicles now account for 19% of exits by value (up from 5% in 2021), and middle-market exits rose to 28.6% of total count as smaller, cleaner deals prove easier to execute.
Why It Matters
Cash distributions — not NAV growth — now determine fundraising success. Firms that show realisations will raise; those relying on valuation marks will struggle.
Signal
Upper-quartile CV MOIC: 1.8x vs 1.6x for comparable buyouts, with a loss ratio of just 9% vs 19%.
Morgan Stanley/Bain via Pantheon, Jan 2026
Implication
Every mid-market sponsor should now have a CV capability — in-house or via a secondaries partner. Valuation quality and focused data analytics are essential to support the equity narrative.

What It Means for PE & Infra Firms

  • Private credit is now core infrastructure — build and maintain direct lender relationships as a strategic priority, not a transactional one
  • Proactive maturity management is essential — sponsors with 2021–22 vintage leveraged assets should act now while refinancing windows remain open
  • CV and structured exit capabilities are no longer optional — the performance data shows they outperform, and LP expectations on distributions are only intensifying
